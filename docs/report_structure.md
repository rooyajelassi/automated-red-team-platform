#Typical red team report structure

-Executive Summary 
This section provides an overview of the engagement including the name of the organization, date of the engagement and the goals achieved. It is written for executives and decision-makers and focuses more on the business impact rather than the technical details.
-Table of Contents
Lists all the sections of the report.
-Methodology and Goals 
This section includes the methodology applied by the red team for this attack and the goals they wanted to achieve. It explains how the attack was conducted and why it reflects realistic threat scenarios.
-Scenario and Scope
Defines the scope of the engagement including domains, IP addresses, subnets, etc. It also outlines the scenarios or plans used to perform the operation.
-Attack Narrative
This section outlines the sequence of events and highlights the key points during the engagement. It includes the evidence collected during the attack such as screenshots, commands, messages, etc.
-Observation and Recommendations
This section is intended to discuss specific scenarios that led the compromise and provides some recommendations to reduce risk and prevent similar attacks in the future.
-Conclusion 
This section includes a summary of the engagement and a conclusion for the entire report.
